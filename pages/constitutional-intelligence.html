Why This Book

AI without constitutional grounding
is a governance failure waiting to happen.

⚠️ The Problem

Government executives face AI decisions without the right tools

Canada's public sector is implementing AI systems faster than governance frameworks can keep pace. The result: accountability gaps, ministerial exposure, fragmented procurement, and wasted investment.

  • Ministerial accountability eroded by opaque algorithmic decisions
  • Billions in AI contracts without constitutional review
  • No common language between technical teams and executives
  • Ethics frameworks that stop at the policy — not the decision
$2–3M
Estimated annual cost per mis-governed AI initiative

This conservative estimate includes rework, legal review, procurement disputes, and reputational cost. Constitutional Intelligence provides the decision architecture to prevent it.

✓ The Solution

Three proprietary frameworks. One coherent architecture.

Constitutional Intelligence offers an integrated decision architecture grounded in the constitutional realities of Westminster parliamentary systems — built specifically for Canadian public servants, executives, and elected officials.

  • IMPACT™ — ministerial accountability impact assessment for AI
  • DECIDE-PLUS™ — structured ethical decision-making for senior leaders
  • FIVE-LAYER™ — governance implementation architecture for AI systems
  • Real case studies drawn from federal government operations
  • Bonus digital content with templates and readiness assessments
Proprietary Frameworks

The tools inside the book

Three interoperable frameworks that together form a complete decision architecture for AI governance in Westminster parliamentary democracies.

IMPACT™
Ministerial Accountability Assessment

Does this AI system preserve ministerial accountability?

A structured assessment tool for evaluating whether an AI initiative protects or erodes the chain of ministerial accountability fundamental to Westminster governance.

  • I Identify the AI decision boundary
  • M Map ministerial accountability flows
  • P Probe for accountability gaps
  • A Assess constitutional risk
  • C Configure oversight mechanisms
  • T Test and document the outcome
DECIDE+™
Ethical Decision Architecture

How do senior leaders make AI decisions responsibly?

An expanded ethical decision framework designed for Deputy Ministers, CIOs, and executives who must navigate AI trade-offs in real time under legal and political constraints.

  • D Define the decision with precision
  • E Examine the ethical landscape
  • C Consult relevant frameworks
  • I Identify rights-holders and risks
  • D Deliberate across stakeholder interests
  • E+ Execute and document accountability
5-LAYER™
Governance Implementation Architecture

How is accountable AI actually built and sustained?

A layered governance architecture that maps accountability requirements across the full AI system lifecycle — from procurement through operation, audit, and decommissioning.

  • 1 Constitutional & Legal Layer
  • 2 Policy & Institutional Layer
  • 3 Organizational & Human Layer
  • 4 Technical & Algorithmic Layer
  • 5 Monitoring & Audit Layer
