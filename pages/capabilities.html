Proprietary Frameworks

The tools inside
Constitutional Intelligence

These frameworks were developed from senior public sector experience and formalized in Constitutional Intelligence (FriesenPress, 2026). They are available to organizations through advisory engagements and the book itself.

DECIDE+™
Methodology

How do senior leaders make AI decisions responsibly?

A decision methodology for evaluating AI choices against constitutional responsibilities. Designed specifically for Westminster systems where legal and democratic accountability are non-negotiable constraints — not optional considerations.

From: Constitutional Intelligence (Ch. 5–7)
FIVE-LAYER™
Architecture

How is accountable AI built and sustained across organizations?

Five structured layers for coordinating AI governance across departments, mandates, and electoral cycles — preventing the siloed failures that cause large-scale implementation disasters in the public sector.

From: Constitutional Intelligence (Ch. 2)
CHARTER RIGHTS DECISION TREE™

Does this AI initiative cause any harm or discrimination against minorities?

A decision path that assesses whether an AI initiative is aligned with constitutional principles, rights protection, and public-sector accountability from the outset - embedding fairness, privacy, transparency, and institutional responsibility as core requirements.

From: Constitutional Intelligence (Ch. 8)
